ANUKAMPA DASAKAM · അനുകമ്പ ദശകം · Ten Verses of Compassion
ഒ-ളി ഒ. ഒ-ളി. ഒ.
ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ!
ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ.
ഒ... ഒ... ഒ... ഒ... ഒ.
One Light, One. One Light. One.
One One One — One One One One — One!
One One One — One One One — One.
One... One... One... One... One.
About this Kriti: Anukampa Dasakam (Ten Verses of Compassion) is a devotional poem expressing the Guru's boundless compassion for all suffering beings. The ten verses move from acknowledging universal suffering to pointing toward the liberation that knowledge brings. The poem is set in the Kakapadham metre and is sung in temples throughout Kerala. Each verse ends with "oru" (one) — a reminder of the underlying unity of all existence.

Verse 1 meaning: The compassionate one sees the suffering of the world and longs to help. But true help comes only from sharing the light of Self-knowledge — for all other help is temporary, while the light of Brahman-knowledge liberates forever.
JANANI PANCHAKAM · ജനനി പഞ്ചകം · Five Verses on the Divine Mother
ഒ-ളി ഒ. ഒ-ളി. ഒ. ഒ. ഒ. ഒ. ഒ.
ജനനീ! ജനനീ! ജനനീ! ഒ!
ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ!
ഒ... ഒ... ഒ.
One Light — One. One Light. One.
Mother! Mother! Mother! One!
One One One — One One One — One!
One... One... One.
About this Kriti: Janani Panchakam consists of five verses celebrating the Divine Mother as the source of all creation and the ultimate refuge of all souls. The Guru composed this at the Subramanya temple and it remains one of the most beloved of his devotional compositions. The Mother here is both the cosmic Mother (Adi Shakti) and the compassionate aspect of the one Supreme Consciousness.
JATI NIRNAYAM · ജാതി നിർണ്ണയം · Declaration on Caste

ജാതി ഭേദ — ഒ!
ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ.
The Most Important Social Declaration: Jati Nirnayam (Determining Caste) is perhaps the most powerful social-philosophical declaration in Indian literature. When asked "What is caste?" the Guru composed these verses to answer definitively:

"Caste is based on birth; it is wrong to discriminate thus. If we say humankind is of one caste by virtue of being born as humans, then the division of caste is meaningless. One who sees another as inferior due to caste is guilty of the sin of discrimination."

This work was revolutionary — it used the tools of Vedantic philosophy to demolish the very foundation of the caste system.
BRAHMAVIDYA PANCHAKAM · ब्रह्मविद्या पञ्चकम् · Five Verses on Knowledge of Brahman
एकं ब्रह्म सर्वत्र वसति।
एकं ज्ञानं एकं प्रकाशम्।
एकं सत्यं एकं चेतनम्।
एकं — ॐ। एकं — ॐ।
One Brahman dwells everywhere.
One knowledge, one light.
One truth, one consciousness.
One — Om. One — Om.
About this Sanskrit Kriti: Brahmavidya Panchakam is composed in Sanskrit in the style of the Upanishads. The five verses present the Guru's vision of Brahma-Vidya (Knowledge of the Absolute) in the most direct terms. This is Advaita Vedanta in its purest form: there is only One existence, one consciousness, one light — and the recognition of this is liberation itself.

Tamil Sacred Songs: These Tamil compositions of the Guru are addressed primarily to devotees in Tamil Nadu and Sri Lanka. They express the same Advaita philosophy in the rich devotional vocabulary of Tamil Shaiva tradition, connecting the Guru's teaching to the lineage of Tamil saints like Thirumoolar and Ramalinga Swami.

Social Reform Writings

Sree Narayana Guru was not merely a philosophical thinker — he was a social revolutionary. These works directly address caste discrimination, religious bigotry, and the need for equality, education, and economic independence.

THE GURU'S EIGHT-POINT PROGRAM
ഒ-ളി ഒ. ഒ-ളി. ഒ.
ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ!
ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ ഒ — ഒ.
ഒ... ഒ... ഒ.
The Guru's Vision for Social Upliftment: In 1924, as part of the Sivagiri Pilgrimage institution, the Guru set out an eight-point program for the spiritual and material upliftment of society:

1. Education — "Vidyakonde Swathantrare Kauka" (Become free through education)
2. Cleanliness — of body, mind, and surroundings
3. Devotion — pure spiritual life
4. Organization — unity of community
5. Agriculture — self-sufficiency
6. Handicraft — skilled labor
7. Technical Training — modern skills
8. Trade — economic independence

Sanskrit Compositions — संस्कृत रचनाएँ

Sree Narayana Guru was a master of Sanskrit. His Sanskrit compositions are remarkable for their fidelity to classical style while expressing his uniquely humane and universalist vision.

ADVAITA DEEPIKA · अद्वैत दीपिका
अद्वैतं शिवम् अद्वयम्।
ब्रह्म सत्यं जगन्मिथ्या।
जीवो ब्रह्मैव नापरः।
एकम् — ॐ। एकम् — ॐ।
Translation: Non-dual, auspicious, without a second. Brahman is real; the world is appearance. The individual Self is none other than Brahman. One — Om. One — Om.

This work is a Sanskrit summary of the entire Advaita philosophy, stated with remarkable conciseness.
DARSANAMALA — ദർശനമാല (Overview)
अधिष्ठान दशकम्। भान दशकम्।
मायान दशकम्। ईश्वर दशकम्।
जीव दशकम्। जगत् दशकम्।
मुक्ति दशकम्। भक्ति दशकम्।
योग दशकम्। ज्ञान दशकम्।
About Darsanamala: Ten Decads (10 × 10 = 100 Sanskrit verses) presenting ten different philosophical visions (Darsanas). Each decad of 10 verses presents a complete philosophical system — from the standpoint of the substratum (Adhisthana Darsana) to Knowledge (Jnana Darsana). Together they represent an encyclopedic tour through Indian philosophy, synthesized in the Guru's Advaita framework.
