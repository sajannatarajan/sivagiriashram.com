Sivagiri Teertha Yatra

ശിവഗിരി തീർത്ഥ യാത്ര
December 30 – January 1

The most sacred pilgrimage associated with Sree Narayana Guru — inaugurated by Guru himself in 1904 at Sivagiri Mutt, Varkala. Each year, hundreds of thousands of pilgrims gather to honour Guru's teachings and renew their commitment to his principles of education, cleanliness, and devotion.

About Sivagiri Pilgrimage

The Sivagiri Teertha Yatra is unique among Indian pilgrimages — it was founded not to honour ancient tradition, but as a deliberate act of social reformation. Guru established the pilgrimage as an alternative to the caste-discriminatory pilgrimages of his time, welcoming all people regardless of birth.

Unlike most Hindu pilgrimages that emphasise physical penance, the Sivagiri pilgrimage emphasises knowledge, cleanliness, and devotion — the three pillars Guru prescribed for social transformation. Pilgrims are encouraged to read, reflect, and discuss Guru's teachings during the journey.

The Three-Day Programme

30
December 30 — Arrival & Flag Hoisting
Dawn

Pilgrims arrive at Sivagiri Mutt. The yellow flag (the colour of knowledge and devotion) is ceremonially hoisted. The Three Flags of the Sivagiri Pilgrimage symbolise Jnana (knowledge), Bhakti (devotion), and Karma (righteous action).

30
December 30 — Inaugural Session
Morning – Afternoon

Grand inaugural ceremony with SNDP leaders, religious scholars, and social activists. Keynote addresses on Guru's relevance to contemporary issues. Recitation of Guru's works.

31
December 31 — Procession & Discourses
All Day

Grand procession of pilgrims through Varkala town to Sivagiri Mutt. Pilgrims wearing the traditional yellow robe (representing knowledge and light). Seminars, discourses, and cultural programmes throughout the day.

1
January 1 — Guru Pooja & Valedictory
Morning & Evening

Special Guru Pooja at the Mahasamadhi Shrine. Valedictory ceremony with cultural programmes and awards for social service. The yellow flag is lowered and pilgrims take their leave with renewed commitment to Guru's ideals.

Sree Narayana Guru Jayanti

ശ്രീ നാരായണ ഗുരു ജയന്തി
Chingam Chathayam — August/September

The birth anniversary of Sree Narayana Guru is celebrated on the star Chathayam in the Malayalam month of Chingam (August–September). It is a public holiday in Kerala and celebrated with great fervour across the state, the country, and wherever Keralites live around the world.

Guru Jayanti Celebrations

1
Prabhath Pheri (Dawn Procession)
5:00 AM

Communities march through streets before dawn, singing Guru's kritis and social-reform songs. The pre-dawn darkness symbolises the ignorance that Guru's wisdom dispelled.

2
Special Guru Pooja
6:00 AM

Grand Guru Pooja with full ritual honours at Sivagiri and all Narayana Guru temples. Guru Stuti, Guru Ashtakam, and Atmopadesa Satakam verses are recited. Prasadam distributed to all.

3
Cultural Programmes
Morning

Cultural programmes celebrating Guru's life — drama, music, dance, recitation competitions. Schools conduct essay competitions on Guru's teachings. SNDP branches organise community meals open to all.

4
Seminars & Discourses
Afternoon

Academic seminars on Guru's philosophy and its contemporary relevance. Leaders and thinkers speak on social justice, education, and Guru's contribution to modern Kerala.

5
Samooha Bhojanam (Community Feast)
Noon

A community feast open to all — symbolising Guru's teaching of universal brotherhood. People of all faiths and communities share food together, breaking caste and religious barriers.

🕯️ Guru Mahasamadhi Dinam

Karkidakam Karthika — September 20, 1928

The Mahasamadhi of Sree Narayana Guru is observed with solemnity and devotion each year. Guru departed from his physical body on Karthika star in the Malayalam month of Karkidakam (September 1928).

Devotees observe Mahasamadhi Dinam with special poojas at the Mahasamadhi shrine at Sivagiri, prayer meetings, recitation of Guru's works, and a day of reflection on Guru's eternal teachings.

ജീവൻ ജ്ഞാനിയ്ക്ക് ജ്ഞാനമേ ദേഹം
ദേഹം ദൈവ ക്ഷേത്രമേ ആകും
"For the wise one, knowledge itself is life; the body is the temple of the divine." — Guru's teaching on the eternal nature of the Self that does not die with the body.

Other Sacred Observances

Aruvippuram Utsavam

February/March — Shivratri

Annual festival at Aruvippuram temple commemorating the historic 1888 consecration. Special Shivratri poojas, procession, and re-enactment of the historic event that changed Kerala.

Guru Purnima

Ashadha Purnima — July

The traditional Guru Purnima festival celebrated at all Narayana Guru temples. Disciples honour the Guru-disciple relationship and the lineage of wisdom through the ages.

Onam Celebrations

August/September — Thiruvonam

Guru believed in Onam as a festival of universal brotherhood. All Narayana Guru institutions celebrate Onam with community feasts open to all, cultural programmes, and recitations of Guru's works.

Vishu Celebrations

April — Vishu Kani

The Malayalam New Year celebrated with special poojas, Kani darshan, and community programmes. Guru's teaching of prosperity through righteous action is the theme of Vishu observances.

SNDP Yogam Foundation Day

May 15

Anniversary of the founding of the SNDP Yogam in 1903 — the organisation Guru blessed to carry forward his social mission. Celebrated with community programmes and renewed commitment to equality.

School Anniversaries

Throughout the year

Anniversaries of schools founded under Guru's inspiration — celebrated as tributes to his vision of universal education. Alumni gather and new scholarships are announced for deserving students.

How to Observe Guru Jayanti at Home

A Devotee's Guru Jayanti

🌅
Morning: Puja and Prayers

Light a lamp (nilavilakku) before Guru's photo or idol. Offer flowers, incense, and fruits. Recite the Guru Stuti, Guru Ashtakam, and selected verses from Atmopadesa Satakam.

📖
Morning: Reading

Read from Guru's works — at least one passage from Atmopadesa Satakam or Darsanamala. Reflect on its meaning and how to apply it in daily life.

🍛
Noon: Community Meal

Share a meal with neighbours, friends, and family — ideally including people from different backgrounds, living out Guru's teaching of universal brotherhood and equality.

🎵
Evening: Music and Recitation

Gather family members to recite or listen to Guru's kritis. Songs like Anukampa Dasakam, Daivadasakam, and other compositions create a devotional atmosphere that connects with Guru's spirit.

🙏
Evening: Commitment

Conclude with a personal commitment to at least one of Guru's principles — to educate oneself, to treat all people equally, to practice cleanliness, or to serve one's community in some way.
