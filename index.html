From Chempazhanthy to Sivagiri — The Divine Journey of Sree Narayana Guru

Born in 1856 in Chempazhanthy, Thiruvananthapuram, Sree Narayana Guru rose from humble origins to become one of India's greatest spiritual luminaries. His life of intense tapas, miraculous consecrations, and tireless social reform transformed Kerala's fabric forever.

Core Message

The Three Pillars of Sree Narayana Guru's Teaching

His life's mission rested on three inseparable foundations — each illuminating and supporting the others, forming a complete path for human transformation.

Explore All Teachings →
01
Vidya (Education)

"Education for liberation, not for employment." Guru founded schools and encouraged learning as the path to self-realisation and social equality.

02
Shuchitvam (Cleanliness)

Purity of body, mind, and environment as spiritual practice. Guru taught that external cleanliness reflects inner clarity of consciousness.

03
Daivabhakti (Devotion)

Heartfelt devotion to God, free of caste discrimination. All are equal before the Divine — this was his revolutionary spiritual declaration.
