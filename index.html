THE ETERNAL TEACHING

One Caste, One Religion,
One God for Humanity

Sree Narayana Guru — philosopher, poet, spiritual leader, and social reformer — arose from the soil of Kerala in the 19th century like a divine light. He taught that all human beings are equal, that caste distinctions are artificial, and that the path to liberation lies in knowledge, devotion, and compassionate action.

His teachings transcend all barriers of religion, language, and community. Through his kritis, philosophical works, and the temples he consecrated, he created a spiritual revolution that transformed Kerala's social fabric and continues to guide millions across the world.

DIVINE LIFE

Key Moments in His Sacred Journey

1856
Born in Chempazhanthy, Thiruvananthapuram on the auspicious Chathayam nakshatra
1888
Consecrated Shiva Linga at Aruvippuram — a revolutionary act breaking caste barriers in temple worship
1903
Established Sivagiri Mutt at Varkala — the spiritual headquarters of his transformative mission
1916
Met Mahatma Gandhi at Varkala — Gandhi called him a "saint among saints and a great social reformer"
1928
Attained Mahasamadhi on September 20 at Sivagiri — merged with the eternal Brahman
