മലയാളം — Malayalam

ഗുരുവിന്റെ മാതൃഭാഷ — The Mother Tongue of the Guru

Malayalam was Sree Narayana Guru's primary language of composition. Most of his devotional, philosophical, and social reform poems were composed in Malayalam — including the Atmopadesa Satakam (100 Verses of Self-Instruction), Anukampa Dasakam, Janani Panchakam, and many more.

Core Teaching

ഒരു ജാതി ഒരു മതം ഒരു ദൈവം മനുഷ്യന്
One Caste, One Religion, One God for Humanity

Atmopadesa Satakam — Verse 1

ഒന്നേ ഉള്ളൂ ഉണ്ടതിനൊരു ഭേദം
ഉണ്ടെന്ന് ഉള്ളതൊ ഇല്ലെന്ന്
ഉള്ളതൊ ഇല്ലെന്ന് ഉണ്ടു ഉഭയ
ഭ്രാന്ത് ഉണ്ടു ഒന്നതിൻ ആദി
"There is only one thing; it seems to be many. Whether this 'seeming many' truly exists or not — that is the perplexity of duality. The origin of this confusion is the One itself."

Daivadasakam — Prayer to God

ഒന്നാണ് ദൈവം, ഒന്നിടം ഇരിക്കൊണ്ടതൊ
ഒന്നല്ലൊ ഈ ജനം ഒന്നിക്കേ കാണ്ക
"God is One, dwelling in one place — yet this people, though one, do not see their unity."

Major Works in Malayalam

ആത്മോപദേശ ശതകം

Atmopadesa Satakam — 100 Verses on the Self. The supreme philosophical poem in Malayalam.

അനുകമ്പ ദശകം

Anukampa Dasakam — Ten Verses on Compassion. Universal love for all beings.

ജനനി പഞ്ചകം

Janani Panchakam — Five Verses on the Divine Mother. Celebrating the feminine divine.

ദൈവദശകം

Daivadasakam — Ten Verses on God. A prayer to the formless divine that transcends all religion.

தமிழ் — Tamil

இறையன்பின் மொழி — Language of Divine Love

Sree Narayana Guru composed several important devotional poems in Tamil, reflecting his deep connection to the Tamil-speaking devotees of Kerala and Tamil Nadu. His Tamil compositions include Thiruvarutpa (Sacred Grace Songs) and devotional verses to Murugan and Shiva.

Core Teaching in Tamil

ஒரு சாதி ஒரு மதம் ஒரு தெய்வம் மனிதன்
One Caste, One Religion, One God for Humanity

Thiruvarutpa — Sacred Grace Song

அன்பே சிவம், அன்பே தெய்வம்
அன்பினால் அனைத்தும் ஒன்றாகும்
காதலே கடவுள், காதலே கடமை
காதலில் கலர்ந்திட வாருங்கள்
"Love is Shiva, Love is God — through love, all becomes one. Love is God, love is duty — come, merge in love."

Tamil Prayer

சாதி இல்லை, மதம் இல்லை, கடவுள் ஒருவரே
மனிதன் மனிதனுக்கு சகோதரன் — இதுவே நன்மையின் வழி
"No caste, no religion, one God — humanity is brotherhood — this alone is the way of goodness."

Major Works in Tamil

திருவருட்பா

Thiruvarutpa — Sacred Grace Songs. Devotional poems in classical Tamil tradition.

முருகன் கீர்த்தனை

Murugan Keerthanai — Songs to Lord Murugan, connecting to the deep Tamil devotional tradition.

संस्कृतम् — Sanskrit

देववाणी — Language of the Gods

Guru's command of Sanskrit was extraordinary for a non-Brahmin in 19th-century Kerala. His Sanskrit compositions include the monumental Darsanamala (Garland of Visions) — ten philosophical decads that demonstrate mastery of the classical philosophical tradition — as well as Brahmavidya Panchakam and many other works.

Darsanamala — Opening Verse

एकमेव अद्वितीयं ब्रह्म
नेह नानास्ति किञ्चन
आदावन्ते च मध्ये च
ह्यनन्तं ब्रह्म केवलम्
"Brahman alone exists, without a second. Here, there is no multiplicity whatsoever. At the beginning, at the end, and in the middle — Brahman alone, infinite."

Brahmavidya Panchakam

ब्रह्म सत्यं जगन्मिथ्या
जीवो ब्रह्मैव नापरः
ज्ञानाद् ब्रह्म विद् ब्रह्मैव भवति
"Brahman is real, the world is illusion, the individual self is Brahman alone — not other. Through knowledge, the knower of Brahman becomes Brahman itself."

Major Works in Sanskrit

दर्शनमाला

Darsanamala — 10 philosophical decads (100 verses). The supreme philosophical masterpiece.

ब्रह्मविद्या पञ्चकम्

Brahmavidya Panchakam — Five Verses on the Knowledge of Brahman.

आत्मविलास

Atmavilasa — Sport of the Self. A meditation on the playful nature of consciousness.

अद्वैत दीपिका

Advaita Deepika — Lamp of Non-Duality. Illuminating the path of Advaita Vedanta.

हिन्दी — Hindi

राष्ट्रभाषा में गुरु का संदेश — Guru's Message in the National Language

While Hindi was not Guru's primary language of composition, his teachings have been translated and disseminated widely in Hindi. His universal message transcends language — and the SNDP and Narayana Guru institutions actively spread his wisdom to Hindi-speaking communities across India.

मुख्य शिक्षाएं — Core Teachings

एक जाति, एक धर्म, एक ईश्वर — मानव के लिए।
बिना जाति-भेद, बिना धर्म-द्वेष — सभी भाई-बहन बनकर जिएं।
"One Caste, One Religion, One God — for Humanity. Without caste division, without religious hatred — all should live as brothers and sisters."

आत्मोपदेश शतक — हिन्दी अनुवाद

एक ही है सत्य, उसमें भेद का भ्रम है
जो है वह है, जो नहीं वह भी है — यही माया का रहस्य
यह सब एक ही के विस्तार में है
"There is only one truth; the appearance of multiplicity is illusion. What exists, exists; what does not exist, also appears to exist — this is the mystery of Maya. All this is within the expansion of the One."

गुरु की प्रमुख शिक्षाएं

  • शिक्षा से स्वतंत्र बनो; संगठित हो; उद्योग से समृद्धि प्राप्त करो
  • मनुष्य को मनुष्य को कष्ट नहीं देना चाहिए — यही सभी धर्मों का सार है
  • ईश्वर मंदिर में नहीं, हृदय में है
  • केवल एक जाति है — वह है मानव जाति
  • जो धर्म भी मानो, अच्छा इंसान बनना काफी है

ಕನ್ನಡ — Kannada

ಗುರುವಿನ ವಾಣಿ — Words of the Guru

Guru's influence extended into Karnataka, particularly in the coastal Tulu Nadu region. Several devotional poems attributed to Guru's tradition have been rendered in Kannada, and the Kannada-speaking community reveres him as a universal saint.

ಮೂಲ ಬೋಧನೆ — Core Teaching

ಒಂದೇ ಜಾತಿ ಒಂದೇ ಧರ್ಮ ಒಂದೇ ದೇವರು ಮನುಷ್ಯನಿಗೆ
"One Caste, One Religion, One God for Humanity"

ಗುರುವಿನ ಶಿಕ್ಷಣ — Guru's Teachings in Kannada

  • ವಿದ್ಯೆಯಿಂದ ಸ್ವತಂತ್ರರಾಗಿ; ಸಂಘಟಿತರಾಗಿ; ಕಾರ್ಯದಿಂದ ಸಮೃದ್ಧಿ ಪಡೆಯಿರಿ
  • ಮನುಷ್ಯರು ಮನುಷ್ಯರನ್ನು ಪೀಡಿಸಬಾರದು — ಇದೇ ಎಲ್ಲಾ ಧರ್ಮಗಳ ಸಾರ
  • ದೇವರು ಒಬ್ಬ, ಜನ ಒಬ್ಬ — ಭೇದ ಏಕೆ?
  • ಒಂದೇ ಒಂದು ಜಾತಿ — ಅದು ಮಾನವ ಜಾತಿ

Guru's Connection to Karnataka

Guru visited Mangalore and other parts of coastal Karnataka during his travels. His message of universal brotherhood resonated deeply with the Kannada-speaking coastal communities, many of whom faced similar caste discrimination. Several SNDP branches operate in Karnataka today.

English

The Universal Message — For the World

Guru's teachings were ahead of their time — and as relevant to the contemporary global world as they were to 19th-century Kerala. Translated and studied in English, his philosophy offers a vision of human unity and spiritual liberation that transcends all cultural and linguistic barriers.

Core Teaching

"One Caste, One Religion, One God for Humanity"
The supreme declaration — in its English rendering, as powerful as in the original Malayalam.

Atmopadesa Satakam — Verse 1 (English)

"There is but one reality; in it there seems to be diversity. Whether this diversity truly exists or not — that is the bewilderment of duality. At the source of this bewilderment is the One itself."

Guru's Message for the World

  • Whatever your religion — being a good human being is sufficient
  • Through education, attain freedom; organise; through industry, achieve prosperity
  • No human being should harm another — this is the essence of all Dharma
  • God is one; humanity is one; why this sense of division?
  • The divine you seek in the temple is your own true nature
  • Service to human beings is the highest form of worship
