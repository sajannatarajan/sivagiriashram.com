BIOGRAPHY

ജീവചരിത്രം

The sacred life of Sree Narayana Guru — philosopher, mystic, poet, and the greatest social reformer Kerala has ever known.

Early Life (1856 – 1876)

Sree Narayana Guru was born on August 22, 1856 (Malayalam year 1032, Chingam month, Chathayam nakshatra) in the village of Chempazhanthy, near Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. He was born into the Ezhava community, which was subjected to severe caste discrimination at the time.

His father, Madan Asan, was a farmer and Ayurvedic practitioner, and his mother, Kuttiyamma, was a devout woman known for her piety. From childhood, the boy — named Nanu — showed extraordinary intelligence, spiritual sensitivity, and compassion for all living beings.

He received traditional education in Sanskrit, Ayurveda, and Malayalam literature from his uncle, Krishnan Vaidyar, and later from the renowned scholar Raman Pillai Asan at Karunagapally. He mastered Sanskrit, logic (Tarka), and Vedanta at a very young age.

Years of Tapas and Spiritual Awakening (1876 – 1888)

In his early twenties, Nanu began a period of intense spiritual seeking. He spent years in deep meditation and tapas (austerities) in caves and forests of Kerala — in Maruthwamala, Aruvippuram, and other sacred spots. During this period he attained the state of a Jnani (one who has realized the Self).

He received the name "Narayana Guru" — meaning "Divine Teacher" — from his disciples and followers. He wandered as an ascetic, teaching through personal example the equality of all human beings before the Divine.

His spiritual realization was rooted in Advaita Vedanta — the non-dual philosophy that sees the one Supreme Consciousness (Brahman) in all beings. This realization became the foundation of his social revolution: if all are manifestations of the same Divine, how can any human being be considered inferior?

LIFE TIMELINE
1856

Birth at Chempazhanthy

Born as Nanu to Madan Asan and Kuttiyamma. The infant showed divine signs — stars aligned on Chathayam nakshatra, an auspicious omen. The village of Chempazhanthy is now a major pilgrimage site.

1870s

Education and Scholarship

Mastered Sanskrit, logic, Vedanta, and Malayalam poetry. Studied under Raman Pillai Asan at Karunagapally. Was known for his extraordinary memory and spiritual depth even as a student.

1888

Aruvippuram Consecration — The Great Revolution

On Shivaratri night, Guru consecrated a Shiva Linga at Aruvippuram on the banks of the Neyyar river. This was a revolutionary act — a person from the "lower" caste performing temple consecration. When questioned, he declared: "This is an Ezhava Shiva." This single act transformed Kerala's social order forever.

1903

Establishment of Sivagiri Mutt

Founded the Sivagiri Mutt at Varkala — a spiritual, educational, and social institution. The Mutt became the nerve center of his reform movement. He also established the Sree Narayana Dharma Paripalana (SNDP) Yogam in 1903 to organize the community.

1904–1914

Prolific Literary and Social Work

Composed numerous kritis, philosophical works, and established dozens of temples open to all castes. His works Atmopadesa Satakam, Darsanamala, and Brahmavidya Panchakam were composed in this period. Temples at Thalassery, Kannur, Thrissur, and across Kerala were consecrated.

1916

Meeting with Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi visited Guru at Sivagiri. Gandhi was deeply moved and later wrote: "I have seen Hindu saints and sadhus, but I have not found among them one who is spiritually greater than Sree Narayana Guru." He called the Guru a "saint among saints."

1924

Vaikom Satyagraha Support

The Vaikom Satyagraha (1924–25) — a campaign for the right of untouchables to use public roads near temples — was inspired by Guru's teachings. Leaders including Gandhiji supported this movement which became a landmark in India's civil rights history.

1928

Mahasamadhi — September 20, 1928

Sree Narayana Guru attained Mahasamadhi on September 20, 1928 at Sivagiri Mutt. His final days were spent in deep meditation and offering blessings to thousands of devotees who came to receive his darshan. His body was interred at Sivagiri, which today is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in Kerala.

His Philosophy in Brief

Guru's philosophy was rooted in Advaita Vedanta — the teaching that all existence is one undivided Consciousness. He expressed this through:

  • Social equality — all humans are manifestations of the one Divine
  • Universal compassion — no discrimination based on caste, religion, or gender
  • Knowledge as liberation — education frees the mind from ignorance
  • Work as worship — honest labor is sacred service
  • Temples open to all — the Divine is not the property of any caste

His Legacy

Sree Narayana Guru's impact on Kerala and India is immeasurable:

  • Over 50 temples and ashrams established across Kerala
  • SNDP Yogam — one of India's largest social organizations
  • Inspired the spread of education among oppressed communities
  • Philosophical works studied worldwide
  • Sivagiri Mutt annually hosts millions of pilgrims
  • Officially honored by the Government of India with commemorative stamps
  • Described by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore as a "great sage"
