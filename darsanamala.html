ദർശനമാല

Darsanamala

Garland of Visions — Ten Philosophical Decads in Sanskrit
The supreme philosophical masterpiece of Sree Narayana Guru

About Darsanamala

Darsanamala (ദർശനമാല — Garland of Visions) is widely regarded as Sree Narayana Guru's greatest philosophical work. Composed in classical Sanskrit, it consists of ten decads (groups of ten verses each) — 100 verses in total — each presenting a different philosophical "vision" or perspective (Darsana) on the nature of ultimate reality.

The ten Darsanas are: Advaita, Deva, Maya, Jiva, Jagat, Karma, Jnana, Bhakti, Yoga, and Nirvana. Each successive Darsana transcends yet incorporates the previous, creating a philosophical spiral that culminates in the vision of Nirvana — the final liberation.

The work demonstrates Guru's profound mastery of Sanskrit, Advaita Vedanta, and the full spectrum of Indian philosophical traditions — from Samkhya to Yoga to Vedanta.

1. Advaita 2. Deva 3. Maya 4. Jiva 5. Jagat 6. Karma 7. Jnana 8. Bhakti 9. Yoga 10. Nirvana
I

Advaita Darsanam

ദ്വൈതാദ്വൈത ദർശനം — Vision of Non-Duality

The first and foundational Darsana establishes the Advaita (non-dual) perspective: Brahman alone is real; the apparent multiplicity of the world is a superimposition on the one reality. The individual self and the universal Self are one.

Verse 1
ഏകമേവാദ്വിതീയം ബ്രഹ്മ
നേഹ നാനാസ്തി കിഞ്ചന
"Brahman alone exists, without a second. Here, there is no multiplicity whatsoever." — The radical proclamation of non-duality: the apparent many are the one Brahman, seen through the veil of Maya.
Verse 2
ആദാവന്തേ ച മദ്ധ്യേ ച
ഹ്യനന്തം ബ്രഹ്മ കേവലം
"At the beginning, at the end, and in the middle — Brahman alone, infinite, without modification." Time and space are appearances within the one infinite consciousness.
Verse 5
സദേവ സോമ്യേദമഗ്ര ആസീദ്
ഏകമേവ അദ്വിതീയം
"In the beginning, dear one, this was Being alone — one only, without a second." The universe's origin in pure Being — echoing the Chandogya Upanishad's profound teaching.
Verse 10
തത്ത്വമസി — അഹം ബ്രഹ്മാസ്മി
അയമാത്മാ ബ്രഹ്മ
"That thou art — I am Brahman — This Self is Brahman." The three great mahavakyas (great sayings) of the Upanishads, affirming the identity of Atman and Brahman.
II

Deva Darsanam

ദേവ ദർശനം — Vision of the Divine

The second Darsana views reality as the manifestation of the divine — the universe as God's own expression, creation as an act of divine delight (Lila). All of existence is sacred.

Verse 1
ദേവോ ദേവ്യൈ നമഃ
ദൈവം ദൈവം ദൈവം
"Salutation to the Goddess in the God — the divine, the divine, the divine." The entire universe is divine — each and every manifestation is an expression of the sacred.
Verse 4
ഈശ്വരഃ സർവഭൂതാനാം
ഹൃദ്ദേശേ അർജുന തിഷ്ഠതി
"The Lord dwells in the heart of all beings." Echoing the Bhagavad Gita — God is not distant but the innermost reality of every being, every particle of existence.
Verse 7
സർവം ഖൽ്വിദം ബ്രഹ്മ
തജ്ജലാൻ ഇതി ശാന്ത ഉപാസീത
"All this indeed is Brahman — from it all is born, into it all returns, by it all breathes. Meditate upon it with calm." The world is not separate from God — it is God's own self-expression.
III

Maya Darsanam

മായ ദർശനം — Vision of Illusion

Maya is the mysterious power by which the one appears as many. This Darsana explores the nature of cosmic illusion — not as falsehood, but as the mysterious veil that conceals the one behind the appearance of multiplicity.

Verse 1
മായാ ഹ്യേഷാ മയാ സൃഷ്ടാ
യൻ മോഹ്യന്തി ജഗൽ ഇദം
"This is my Maya, created by me, by which this entire world is deluded." The divine voice speaks — Maya is not an accident but a divine creative power, the artist's brush with which Brahman paints the universe.
Verse 5
ദൈവീ ഹ്യേഷാ ഗുണമയീ
മമ മായാ ദുരത്യയാ
"This divine Maya of mine, composed of the Gunas, is difficult to transcend." Maya is woven from the three Gunas (Tamas, Rajas, Sattva) — the three fundamental qualities that constitute all of manifest existence.
Verse 9
മാം ഏവ യേ പ്രപദ്യന്തേ
മായാം ഏതാം തരന്തി തേ
"Those who take refuge in me alone — they cross beyond this Maya." Surrender to the divine — or the realisation of one's identity with Brahman — is the way through the veil of Maya.
IV

Jiva Darsanam

ജീവ ദർശനം — Vision of the Individual Soul

The fourth Darsana turns attention to the individual soul (Jiva) — its nature, its relationship to the body and mind, and its ultimate identity with Brahman. The Jiva appears separate but is, in reality, Brahman itself.

Verse 1
ജീവോ ബ്രഹ്മൈവ നാ പര:
ജ്ഞാനാദ് ബ്രഹ്മ വിദ്ബ്രഹ്മൈവ ഭവതി
"The individual soul is Brahman alone — not other. Through knowledge, the knower of Brahman becomes Brahman itself." The Jiva's separation is apparent, not real. Realisation dissolves the apparent boundary.
Verse 3
ദേഹോ ദേവാലയ: പ്രോക്ത:
ജീവോ ദേവ: സനാതന:
"The body is called the temple; the individual soul is the eternal God." A revolutionary teaching — the temple of the divine is not a stone building but the human body itself. The priest of this temple is your own awareness.
Verse 8
ആത്മൈവ ഹ്യാത്മനോ ബന്ധുഃ
ആത്മൈവ രിപുഃ ആത്മന:
"The Self is indeed the friend of the self, and also the enemy of the self." Echoing the Gita — the Self is both the liberator and the confiner, depending on whether it is realised or identified with the ego-mind.
V

Jagat Darsanam

ജഗൽ ദർശനം — Vision of the World

The fifth Darsana views the manifest world — its origin, nature, beauty, and ultimate relation to Brahman. The world is real as appearance, while Brahman is the reality in which the appearance occurs.

Verse 1
ഏകോ ദേവ: സർവഭൂതേഷു ഗൂഢ:
സർവ്വവ്യാപീ സർവഭൂതാന്തരാത്മ
"One God hidden in all beings, all-pervading, the inner soul of all." The world is the manifestation of the hidden divine — every grain of dust, every atom, every star is the one God expressing itself.
Verse 6
അഗ്നിർ ജ്യോതിരഹ: ശുക്ല:
ഷൺ‌മാസ ഉത്തരായണം
"Fire, light, day, the bright half, the six months of the northern path..." The cosmic order — the rhythms of fire and light, day and night, the procession of the seasons — all are expressions of the divine order.
VI

Karma Darsanam

കർമ്മ ദർശനം — Vision of Action

The sixth Darsana explores the law of Karma — action and its consequences, the cycle of cause and effect, and how selfless action (Nishkama Karma) becomes a path of liberation rather than bondage.

Verse 1
ഗഹനാ കർമ്മണോ ഗതി:
ന കർമ്മ ലിപ്യതേ നരേ
"Unfathomable is the way of Karma. Action does not cling to a person." The mysterious depth of the law of Karma — and the promise that the liberated one is not bound by action.
Verse 4
കർമ്മണ്യേവ അധികാരസ്തേ
മാ ഫലേഷു കദാചന
"Your right is to action alone, never to its fruits." The supreme teaching of the Bhagavad Gita on Karma Yoga — act fully and selflessly, offering the fruits to the divine.
Verse 8
ലോകസംഗ്രഹം ഏവ അപി
സമ്പശ്യൻ കർതും അർഹസി
"For the welfare of the world, you should act." Selfless action — not for personal gain but for the benefit of all beings — is itself a form of worship and a path to liberation.
VII

Jnana Darsanam

ജ്ഞാന ദർശനം — Vision of Knowledge

Jnana (knowledge) is not mere information but the transformative realisation of ultimate truth. This Darsana celebrates Jnana as the supreme liberator — the direct knowledge that the Self is Brahman.

Verse 1
ന ഹി ജ്ഞാനേന സദൃശം
പവിത്രം ഇഹ വിദ്യതേ
"Truly, nothing in this world purifies like knowledge." Knowledge (Jnana) is the supreme purifier — not merely intellectual understanding, but the transformative recognition of one's true nature.
Verse 5
ജ്ഞാനം ലബ്ധ്വാ പരാം ശാന്തിം
അചിരേണ അധിഗച്ഛതി
"Having obtained knowledge, one soon attains supreme peace." The fruit of Jnana is not a future heaven but the immediate recognition of the peace that is one's true nature — Sat-Chit-Ananda.
Verse 9
ജ്ഞാനാഗ്നി: സർവകർമ്മാണി
ഭസ്മസാൽ കുരുതേ തഥാ
"The fire of knowledge burns all actions to ashes." Just as fire reduces wood to ash, the fire of Self-knowledge burns away the accumulated Karma that binds the soul to the cycle of rebirth.
VIII

Bhakti Darsanam

ഭക്തി ദർശനം — Vision of Devotion

Bhakti (devotion) is not a lower path but a purification of the heart that dissolves the ego and opens the door to realisation. This Darsana shows that love of the divine and knowledge of the divine are ultimately one.

Verse 1
ഭക്ത്യാ മാം അഭിജാനാതി
യാവൻ ചാസ്മി തത്ത്വത:
"Through devotion one knows me truly — who and what I am in reality." Devotion is not mere emotion but a mode of knowing — the heart's direct recognition of the divine.
Verse 4
മൻ‌മനാ ഭവ മദ്ഭക്തോ
മദ്യാജീ മാം നമസ്കുരു
"Fix your mind on me, be devoted to me, worship me, bow to me." The divine invitation to love — not transactional religion but the melting of the separate self in the recognition of the beloved divine.
Verse 7
ഭക്തിർ ഭഗവതോ ഹ്രദ്യ:
ഭക്ത്യൈവ ലഭ്യ: ഭക്തോ
"Devotion is dear to the Lord — through devotion alone is the devoted one attained." The paradox of devotion — in loving the divine other, the devotee discovers that the "other" is their own deepest Self.
IX

Yoga Darsanam

യോഗ ദർശനം — Vision of Union

Yoga (union) is both the goal and the path — the systematic practice of stilling the mind to reveal the Self that always is. This Darsana presents Yoga as the direct route to realisation.

Verse 1
യോഗ: ചിത്തവൃത്തി നിരോധ:
തദ ദ്രഷ്ടു: സ്വരൂപേ അവസ്ഥാനം
"Yoga is the cessation of the movements of the mind. Then the Seer abides in its own true nature." Patanjali's supreme definition — when the mind is stilled, the pure consciousness of the Self stands revealed.
Verse 5
ഏകാഗ്രം ചേതസ: കൃത്വ:
ആരോഹ ബ്രഹ്മ ലോകം
"Having made the mind one-pointed, ascend to the realm of Brahman." Concentration — the gathering of scattered attention into the single point of pure awareness — is the royal road to realisation.
Verse 9
ഓം തദ്ബ്രഹ്മ, ഓം തദ്വായു:
ഓം തദാത്മ, ഓം തദ്സത്യം
"Om is Brahman, Om is the wind, Om is the Self, Om is Truth." The sacred syllable OM as the sound-symbol of ultimate reality — the Yoga of sound leading to the recognition of the soundless absolute.
X

Nirvana Darsanam

നിർവ്വാണ ദർശനം — Vision of Liberation

The culminating Darsana — Nirvana as the extinction of the false sense of separation and the recognition of infinite, unbounded being. The ultimate Darsana: there is nothing to attain, for liberation is the nature of what we always already are.

Verse 1
നിർവ്വാണം പദം ഈശ്വരം
അക്ഷരം ബ്രഹ്മ ഉത്തമം
"Nirvana is the state of the Lord — the imperishable Brahman, the highest." Nirvana is not annihilation but the recognition of the imperishable — the dropping away of the false limited self to reveal the infinite.
Verse 5
സർവ ദുഃഖ നിവൃത്തൗ ച
ആനന്ദ ഉദയ ഏവ ച
"With the cessation of all suffering, and the arising of bliss." Nirvana as the negative (freedom from suffering) and the positive (the arising of inherent bliss, Ananda) aspects of liberation.
Verse 10 (Final Verse)
ഇദം ജ്ഞാത്വാ വിമുക്ത്യർഥം
ബ്രഹ്മ ഭൂതോ ഭവേൻ നര:
"Knowing this, for the sake of liberation — one becomes Brahman itself." The final teaching of Darsanamala: knowing Brahman, one becomes Brahman. Not a future event — but the recognition of what always is. This is the culmination, the garland of visions complete.
